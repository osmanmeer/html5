2. UMAR IBN KHATTAB RAA

Umar RAA was the second Rightly Guided caliph, he was one of the Companions closest to the Prophet PBUH in his struggle for Unity in an attempt to spread Islam and make it reign in this world. Umar's sobriquet was "Faruq," which means the one who discriminates between right and wrong.

His father was Khattab b. Nufayl and his genealogy meets with the Prophet PBUH in Kab. A member of the Adiw branch of the Quraish tribe, his mother was Hanteme the sister or cousin of Abu Jahl.

Umar RAA was tall with a big frame, light-skinned, a broad forehead, a long mustache and free-falling hair. Being a shepherd to camels in his childhood, Umar RAA was busy with such honorable works among Arabs as learning genealogy and how to use weapons. At the same time Umar RAA was a very good speaker.

Umar RAA was also set up state organizations. He established the Council. In order to put legal matters in order, he was the first person to appoint judges working independently of the governors. In 17 H, he printed money and put it in circulation. He established military headquarter towns in order for armies to be located close to enemy regions.

Umar RAA wished to be a martyr. His wish became a reality when he was stabbed by a non-Muslim slave and he died three days after this event.
