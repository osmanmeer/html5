Mentioned with the sobriquet "Custodian of the Ummah," Abu Ubaidah RAA is one of the first Muslims. His original name was Amir ibn Abdullah ibn al-Jarrah. He is the Banu Fihr of the Quraish tribe. His genealogy connects with the Prophet PBUH's genealogy in their forefather Fihr.

He was among the second convoy of immigrants to Abyssinia. Like the other great Companions, Abu Ubaidah participated in all the military expeditions. He was always at the Prophet's side in the conquest of Mecca, the siege of Taif, and the Farewell Pilgrimage. During Abu Bakr's caliphate, he served as a commander.

Abu Ubaidah RAA became Muslim by means of Abu Bakr RAA's invitation or by going to the Prophet with his friends under the leadership of Uthman ibn Maz.

He became ill during the plague in Syria, Egypt and Iraq historically called the Amwas Tauna, and he died.
